# NEPQ Cold Outreach — Earn the Next 30 Seconds

Cold outreach has one job: lower resistance enough to earn permission for a few questions. You are NOT pitching, NOT claiming value, NOT asking for a meeting on the first line. You're interrupting the prospect's expectation of a salesperson.

## The cold call opener

The prospect is braced for a pitch. Defy the pattern.

1. **Disarming pattern interrupt** — name the reality, lower the guard:
   - "Hey {name}, you're gonna hate me for a second — this is a cold call. Want to hang up, or can I take 30 seconds to tell you why I called and you decide?"
   - (Most people give the 30 seconds because you were honest and gave them control.)
2. **Permission-based reason** tied to their world, phrased as a question:
   - "I work with {ICP type} who are dealing with {common problem}. Out of curiosity — is that something you're running into, or have you got it totally dialed in?"
3. **Move to a situation question** (Stage 2 of the framework) if they engage. Do not pitch.

Tone: calm, slightly detached, unhurried. Lower energy than they expect. Confidence reads as not needing the sale.

## Cold email / DM

Short. One idea. A question, not a pitch. No "I'd love to hop on a call to show you what we do."

Structure:
- **Subject:** curious or specific, never salesy ("quick question about {their area}")
- **Line 1:** relevance — why them, specifically (not "I came across your company")
- **Line 2:** the problem-as-question — "How are you currently handling {problem area}?" or "Is {problem} something you've solved, or still a bit of a headache?"
- **Close:** low-pressure, optional — "No worries if it's handled — just figured I'd ask."

Example:
> Subject: quick question about your {area}
>
> {Name} — I work with {ICP} around {city/space} and {problem} comes up a lot.
> How are you handling that right now — solved, or still a bit of a pain?
> No pressure either way, just curious if it's worth a quick chat.

## Rules for cold outreach

- **Never** open with the offer, a feature, or a "value prop."
- **Never** use false scarcity, fake personalization, or invented social proof.
- One question per message. Let them answer before you advance a stage.
- Respect compliance — `{{outreach_compliance}}` (CAN-SPAM, TCPA, GDPR). Honor opt-outs immediately and never contact a do-not-contact.
- **Sending is approval-gated** unless onboarding enables auto-send (see SOUL.md Autonomy Rules). Draft the sequence; route for approval before it goes to a real prospect.

## Flagship example — property management BD

Selling property management to a rental-property owner/investor:
- Opener: "Hey {name} — cold call, I'll be quick. I work with rental owners around {city}. Are you self-managing your places, or do you already have a manager?"
- If self-managing → situation/problem-awareness: "How's that been going with {number} units — more of a side-headache or fully under control?"
- If has a manager → "Got it. If there were one thing you'd change about how they handle it, what would it be?"

Then run the framework. Never pitch the management fee first — surface the cost of the headache first.
