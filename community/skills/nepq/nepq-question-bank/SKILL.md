# NEPQ Question Bank — The Black Book (Verbatim)

The exact, verbatim question library transcribed from *The NEPQ Black Book of Questions* (Jeremy Miner / 7th Level). The framework sub-skill tells you *which type* of question to ask at each stage; this bank gives you the *precise words*.

**Usage:** identify the stage (`../nepq-framework/`), pull the exact phrasing from the matching section, and adapt only the variable bits (prospect name, their stated problem, the offer). Keep the question structure verbatim — the wording is engineered to lower resistance. Verbal pacing matters: `...` marks a deliberate pause; tone notes (curious / concerned / skeptical / playful / challenging) are part of the technique.

> The full 100-page book (every industry-specific script) is ingested to the `agent-dane` KB collection. Query it for a specific industry: `cortextos bus kb-query "NEPQ <industry> <stage> questions" --org $CTX_ORG --agent dane`. This file carries the generic reusable engine plus representative examples.

## STAGE 1 — CONNECTION

Goal: lower resistance, put focus on them. Never pitch here.

### Connection questions (generic)
- "Have you found what you're wanting, or are you still looking for…?"
- "When you talked with _______, what was it that you guys talked about that caused you… to wanna look into this further?"
- "When you saw the ad that was going over _______, what was it they were talking about… that caused you to want to… look into this further?"
- "And do you know what you're… looking for?"

### Outbound lead opener
- "Hey [Prospect Name], this is just [Your Name]… with [Your Company]… It looks like you recently responded to an ad on [____] and asked us to call you… about possibly helping you with [____]. And I just had a few minutes before my next appointment. I guess I should start off by asking you, have you found what you're looking for, or are you still looking for… [repeat back the end result]?"

(The word "just" implies they should already know you. Use a relaxed, unhurried tone.)

### Inbound lead opener
- "Hey [Prospect Name] this is just [Your Name]… with [Your Company]… it looks like you recently booked on our calendar… about possibly getting help with [their problem] so that you can [repeat back the end result], right?"
- "So when you went through the ad where XYZ was talking, what was it about what they were saying that caused you to… wanna look into this further?"
- "And what were you hoping to get out of the call with us today, just so we have a better understanding?"

### The Status Frame (use right after the opener, on every call)
- "Now, this call is pretty basic; it's really more for us to find out about what you're using now, what results you're getting, compared to what you might be wanting, to see what that GAP looks like… And towards the end of the call, if you feel like it… 'might be'… what you're looking for, we can talk about 'possible' next steps. Would that help you?"

### Industry example — Auto (outbound)
- "Hey [Prospect Name], yea… it's just [Your Name] from [Company Name]. You had asked us to call you back… it looks like you responded to an ad on Facebook this morning about possibly looking at that… Red 2022 A6 Audi, right?"
- "Yea, we just got that car on a trade-in that you were looking at; what aspects of the car, I guess… caused you to maybe… want to test drive it?"
- "Aww, okay, and do you know what type of vehicle you might be looking for… besides the red Audi… just in case it's already gone… by the time you could get down here to look at it?" (builds urgency)

## STAGE 2 — ENGAGEMENT

### 2a. Situation questions (current state — neutral)
- "What are you doing now?" / "What are you using now?"
- "How long have you been…?"
- "What got you involved with…?"
- "Can you walk me through what you're doing now to…?"
- "What type of _______ do you have now, just so I understand?"
- "How many _______ do you have so I have more context?"
- "You mentioned in your application that you're having some issues with _______. Can you tell me a little bit more about that?"
- "What are you doing now to get _______ so I have more background?"

### 2b. Problem-Awareness questions (surface the friction)
Core moves — do they like their situation, and what's the history:
- "Do you like what you're…?" / "Do you like what you…?" / "Do you want to…?"
- "What makes you feel like [Current Provider/Product]'s not enough, though?"

**Two Truths** (when the prospect is guarded / claims everything is fine):
- "So, to me, it sounds like things are going 100% perfect for you. Is there anything you would change about your [present situation], results you're getting from that if you… if you could?"
- (If they admit it's not perfect) "Not 100% perfect?" — then: "So I'm curious: what is it about your _______ that you don't like?" → "Why would you change that?" → "Why is that important to you now, though?"

**Clarifying questions** (slow them down, make them expand):
- "Oh, what do you mean by that?"
- "Can I ask what you mean when you said…?"
- "I'm not sure I quite understand?"
- "When you said… could you expand on that for me?"
- "Let me see if I understand what you want/what you're saying…"

**Precision probing** (deepen the problem):
- "How long has that been going on?"
- "Has that had… has that had an impact on you?" → "In what way, though?"
- "You don't sound so sure about it… what don't you like?"
- "But besides [reason #1] and [reason #2], what's the main reason why you might be looking [your product/service]… rather than [repeat back alternative]?"
- "Can you give me some specific examples of when that happened?"
- "How often does this happen?" → "How does that… impact you when this happens?"
- "Besides you, who else in your department does this impact right now?"
- "What happens if this stays the same…?"

### 2c. Solution-Awareness questions

**Part 1 — past attempts (open the gap on what hasn't worked):**
- "Before we started talking today, were you out there looking for [what they said they want] so you can [the result they wanted]?"
  - If NO: "What's prevented you from doing that in the past?"
  - If YES: "What did you do?" → "How did that work out?" → "What kind of results did you get from it?" → "What do you think held you back from having success…?"
  - If BAD results: "Do you know if other people were getting results from that?" → "Why do you think others were able to get results, but you didn't?"

**Criteria question sequence (bridge to Part 2):**
- "Let's do this… just to make sure that what we're doing could actually help you… besides [Benefit #1] and [Benefit #2], what would you actually be looking for in [your Product/Service]?"
- "Like what would be your ideal criteria?"
- "Can I ask why that's important to you… now though?"
- "Is there anything else you want?"

**Part 2 — future pacing (let them feel the solved state):**
- "So besides [repeat back what they said they wanted], how do you see this benefiting you the most, though?"
- "What would it do for you personally, though?"
- "How would it be different, though? You being able to [what they wanted]. How would your life/business be… maybe different than it is now?"
- "How would that make you… feel though?"
- "Let's say that we are able to [deliver the benefit]… what would that do for you personally?"

### 2d. Consequence questions (cost of doing nothing — challenging/concerned tone)
- "What if you don't do anything about this problem [their problem], and your situation gets even worse?"
- "Have you thought about what would happen if you don't do anything about this?"
- "What are you going to do if nothing changes, if you keep using/doing [their problem] for the next [3, 6, 12 months / 5, 10, 15 years] and nothing changes?"
- (After a negative response) "Do you want to have to go through all of that… if you… if you didn't have to?"
- (Tougher) "Are you willing to settle for that, though? Well, whose choice is it if you settle or not?"
- "So why look at [solving this] now? Why not push it down the road like a lot of [people/companies] who [negative outcome]?"

### 2e. Qualifying questions (confirm importance + commitment to change)
- "How important is this issue to you?"
- "How important is it for you to change your situation and start…? [repeat back what they want]"
- "How important is it for you to do something about this? Why now, though?"
- "Is it important to you to solve this problem? Why?"
- "Are you prepared… to change your situation so you can…? [what they said they wanted]"
- "It sounds like you're frustrated with… [what they don't like]. So, if you could get what you want, how important is it for you to do that? Why now?"

Qualifying starter phrases: "Let's pretend we could…" / "What if…" / "Just suppose we could…" / "Imagine for a moment…" / "Assuming we were able to…"
…closed with: "What do you feel would happen next?" / "What do you see happening next?" / "Where would we go from there?"

## STAGE 3 — TRANSITION

Use this formula (bridge from their problem to your solution, with permission):
- "Based on what you told me… what we are doing could/would actually work for you…"
- "Because you know how you said… [repeat back what they said they wanted]"
- "And because of that, it's making you feel… [repeat back the emotion: frustrated, stressed, worried, concerned]…"

Bridging to the presentation:
- "With your permission, I can go over a few things that we would be able to do for you to help you with your situation and solve those problems [the actual problems]. Would that help you?"
- (2-call / B2B) "Good first call; that gives me more understanding of your situation… Really, the next step would be to book another call to go over a demo… Would that help you?"

## STAGE 4 — PRESENTATION

Keep it to ~10–15% of the call. Present only against the problems they named.

### The 3-Step Presentation Formula
1. "One of the biggest problems that people have when they come to us is they don't know how to/are having issues with… [their main problem]. Which causes them to… [the consequences]."
2. "So the way we solve that for our clients like you is we… [how your solution solves that part]."
3. "And what that means to you is… [the advantages and benefits once it's solved]."

### Checking-for-Agreement questions (ask 8–12 throughout the presentation)
- "Does that make sense?" / "Are we on the same page?" / "Are you with me on this?"
- "What are your thoughts on that?"
- "Do you see how that works?" / "Do you see how that could help you?"
- "How do you see that helping you the most?"
- "Any questions about that?"

### Proposal questions (generic)
- "We had planned to cover the following areas [the areas] for you. In your mind, what would be the most important thing you'd want to spend more time on?"
- "What are your thoughts on what we have outlined so far?"
- "In what ways does this capture what you're trying to accomplish?"
- "Can you walk me through your decision-making process?"
- "How will your funding/budget to solve this problem be determined?"
- "Who besides you would be deciding on which firm to work with?"
- "I sense you are hesitant about what we have covered. Can you help me understand what is behind that?" (concerned tone)
- "What's holding you back?" (concerned tone)
- (If they're shopping competitors) "Let's say you meet with these other companies, and it seems like they can all solve the [XYZ problems] like we can, and the pricing is somewhat similar; how would you then decide who to go with?"
- "Is there anything else you'd like to go over before we finalize this so you can…? [what they said they wanted]"

### Proposal-upfront rule (when asked for a proposal before discovery)
- "Yea, for sure, I'd be open to putting together a proposal for you, but just so you know, I'm not quite sure we could even help you yet. It might be better if we ask a few questions about your situation, and then put something together that might be more useful. Would it help you if we did that for you?"

## STAGE 5 — COMMITMENT

Let them commit themselves — ask, don't push.
- "Do you feel like this could be the… answer for you?"
- (Always follow with) "Why do you feel like it is, though?" / "Why do you feel it would, though?"
- "What specific parts/aspects of what we went over today do you feel… are going to really help you the most?"
- "Do you feel like this is something you can have/do that will get you where you want to go?"
- (Money objection mid-commit) "Money aside, why do you feel like it is, though?" (get the "yes" before handling concerns)

Closing into next steps:
- "Well, it looks like we covered what you're possibly looking for in _______. Really, the next step is we would make some [the next steps] type of arrangement for you _______; you can use a credit or debit card, invoice, wire transfer, then at that point we would _______ so that you can [repeat back what they said they wanted]. Would that be appropriate, or how… would you like to proceed from here?"

Multi-option close (e.g. 3 tiers):
- "Which one of those would you… lean towards?" (soft tone)
- "How come that one, just so I understand?"

## COLD CALLING (Bonus Section)

### Opener
- "Is [Prospect Name] there?"
- "Hey [Prospect Name], this is just [Your Name]. I was wondering if you could… help me out for a moment?" (slightly confused tone)
- "Well, I'm not quite sure who I should be talking to; I called to see who would be responsible for looking at any possible hidden gaps in your _______ that could be causing you to _______. Who should I be talking to about that?"
- (If they get aggressive) "Oh, I apologize if I offended you. What I do is… you know how… [personalized intro]."

### Gatekeeper / voicemail
- "Should I have you transfer me over to their voicemail to leave a message so they can call back later if they'd like?"

### Personalized intro (30-second commercial)
- "Well, you know how a lot of people/companies sometimes get frustrated with _______, _______, and _______? Well, what I do is, I help people/companies like that with _______, _______ and _______. Does that resonate with you/your company or something you might be experiencing?"

## OBJECTION HANDLING (diffuse with a question — never rebut)

### "Send me references"
- "Yea, I can do that for sure… Now, just so I can send the right people to you, what specifically would you like to discuss with them?"
- "When do you plan on calling them so I can let them know… to see if they would be available for you?"
- "Now, let's pretend for a moment that the clients you talk to say good things about how we were able to solve the same type of problems you're having… where do you feel we should go from there?"

### "Money / no budget" (funding sequence)
- "Tell me, if you did have the funding/budget/money, is this something that would work for you?"
- "I can appreciate that money might be an issue based on what you told me. How do you think you can resolve that… so you can find the funding so that you can…? [what they want]"
- "What other avenues do you have to find the funds so that you can…? [what they want]"
- "Can I make a suggestion? What I can do is show you the other avenues our clients used to find the funds if they didn't have them themselves, and you'll have to see if you have those avenues… Which of those avenues do you have so that you can solve this XYZ problem and…? [what they want]"

### "I want to go another direction / wait" (use sparingly, skeptical tone)
- "Can I ask you something? … How can I communicate to you… that you might be making a mistake… without you getting upset with me?"
- (Advanced, skeptical tone only) "Why am I even here?" / "Why did you even want to see me?"

## EXPANDED REFERENCE BANKS (Bonus Section)

### Pre-Situation questions (rapport + context)
- "How long have you been with the company? How has your job evolved since you started?"
- "What do you like about your work here? What don't you like, if anything?" (playful)
- "What would your customers say is the biggest reason they do business with you over someone else? Why do you feel that is?"

### Past-Situation questions
- "What are some of the hurdles you've had in the past to solve [the problem]?"
- "Can you give me an example of something recent in which you had to deal with _______ problem?"
- "What's been the toughest thing you've had to deal with recently? Tell me more…"
- "If you could do this over again [the issue], what would you do differently from before?"

### Boardroom / meeting openers
- "From your perspective, what would be a valuable way for us to spend this time together?"
- "What prompted you to want to have this meeting today with us so we have more context?"
- "How did you reach the decision to possibly seek outside help to come in?"
- "What are the most important items you'd like for us to cover today? Oh, why those items, just so I understand?"

### Problem-Awareness (expanded, B2B-leaning)
- "I mean, you've been with XYZ vendor for ___ years; it can't be ALL doom and gloom — what do you like about what they've done? … What would you change if you could, though?"
- "Would you be opposed to sharing the top two challenges you're having now? Of these two, which one is most important to solve?" → "Why is it so important to you now, though?"
- "What's causing the problem? Tell me more…"
- "What barriers are stopping you from overcoming this?"
- "How much do you feel this problem is costing you in lost revenue/sales?"
- "If you could change one thing about your current vendor/company, what would that be? Why is changing that important to you, though?"
- "How is the problem affecting your bottom line? In what way?"

### Precision-probing lead-ins
- "Tell me more… about that?" (curious)
- "Can you give me a specific example so I can understand this better?"
- "Describe for me what you meant by that?"
- "Can you unpack that for me a bit more…"
- "Explain that to me in more detail just so I understand…"
- "How do you mean exactly?"
- "What's prompting you to look into changing this?"
- "Why is this important to you now, though?"
- "What would it mean for you to be able to solve this problem?"
- "Which of these problems are impacting you the most? Why this one, though?"

### Decision-making questions
- "Can you walk me through the criteria you use when choosing a company… to solve these issues you talked about?"
- "Can you walk me through your company's decision-making process… when it comes to solving problems like this?"
- "You told me your company has budgeted _______ to fix this problem. Can I ask… how that was determined?"
- "Do you feel the budget/funding you've been given is sufficient to solve XYZ problem?"
- "Who else besides you would be involved in approving the funding/budget to solve this?"
- "How will you get financial approval from the company to solve this XYZ issue?"
- "Let's suppose you meet with all these other companies, and let's say they all meet your criteria… can solve the same problems like we can… and pricing is somewhat similar… How would you then decide who to go with at that point?"

### Funding / budgeting questions
- "Could you go over your funding/budgetary process when it comes to solving problems like this?"
- "Just to see if I could help you, what funding/budgetary parameters are you working with?"
- "What obstacles would you possibly encounter when trying to get financial approval to solve XYZ problem?"
- "What type of funding/funds do you have to put into this so you can start _______? [what they wanted]"

### Vendor-disruption questions
- "Can I ask how your ideal situation compares to what you have now with this vendor you're using?"
- "Back when you chose to work with that company, what were your selection criteria? In what ways has that changed as you look at your situation today, though?"
- "So, to me, it sounds like things are 100% PERFECT with what you're using now; what would you change if you could?"
- "Can I ask in what ways your vendor could do better for you than what they are doing now, though?"

### Questions about the prospect's own customers (B2B)
- "Can you go over who your most valuable customers are? What's the typical customer look like for you?"
- "How would your clients measure success from doing business with you? What would they expect so they don't go somewhere else?"
- "What are the main reasons some of your prospects go with your competitors over you? Has that had an impact on you? Do you want to change that?"
- "Have you lost clients because of these issues? How much were those clients worth to you financially?"

### Consequence (expanded, B2B)
- "If you don't decide to address this problem now… how much will it cost you this year… in lost profit/revenue/sales?"
- "If you don't solve this issue, what's the impact on your business? Can you afford to take that risk?"
- "What's the potential impact on your business if you don't do anything about this? Are you willing to settle for that?"

### Outcome / "what would it mean to you" questions (powerful for getting to emotion)
- "What would this do for you… personally to solve [their problem]? Would it?"
- "How serious would you say this issue is for you?" (lean in, concerned tone)
- "If there was a way to eliminate this problem… what effects would that have on your company? In what way?"
- "If you came to your CEO with a solution that could save the company around [$X] a year, what would that mean for you… personally? What would they say?"
- "If you were able to accomplish this objective [their objective], what would that do for you personally? In what way, though?"

## OBJECTION — "Send information to my email"

- (Prospect) "I'm just really busy today. Can you just send some information to my email, and I'll get back to you?"
- (You) "Yea, I can do that for sure, now, just so I can put together the right information for you. What exactly are you looking for?"
- (They answer) "Okay, and who/what do you use now for your…?" → now run the Engagement Stage from the first Situation question.
- (Near the end, circling back) "Okay, good first call, that helps us better understand what's going on. What I can do, if you'd like, is send you the info about how we could solve [XYZ problems they mentioned]. Now, let's suppose you and the firm go through the details we sent over and see if it fits what you're looking for. What would you want… the next step to be?"

> Rule: never send information/a proposal before finding out if this is a serious person wanting to change their situation.

## NEPQ CALENDAR COMMITMENT — "Call me back / I'm too busy"

Don't accept a vague "call me back" (you'll chase a ghost). Flip it so they commit:
- (Prospect) "Can you call me back? I'm too busy right now."
- (You) "That's not a problem. What I can do if it helps you… I'll give you my number, and you'll have to call me back later today to see if I would be available for you. Would that help? My number is [____]. What's your timeframe on getting back to me today, just to see if I would even be in the office for you?"
- (If they say "later this week") "Well, possibly, it might be harder to randomly get a hold of me with my schedule. Here's what I can do though: if you have your calendar handy, I could pull out mine so you can book a specific time with me; that way you don't have to chase me down and vice versa… Would that work for you?"

(Positions you as the busy expert whose time is valuable. If they no-show the booked time, wait two minutes, then call: "Hey [Name], it's just [You] getting back to you, I'm just a little bit behind schedule…")

## REFERRALS (how to ask and actually get them)

Ask only after you've delivered/closed. The sequence:
1. "I appreciate the opportunity to be able to help you. Can I ask you… in your mind, how do you feel we've been able to help you the most?" (they tell *themselves* the value and own it)
2. "With that in mind, who do you know that might be struggling with… [the problem you solved]?"
3. "Can you tell me a little bit more about this person and why you feel I could help them, though?"
4. "Well, how do you think it would be best to approach them?" (find the channel)
5. (If they say "why don't you call him?") "Well, I guess I could. Do you feel like you should communicate to him first that I'll be calling, though?"
6. "What do you think you should say?" → then "Can I make a suggestion? What if you talked about some of the challenges you had that he's having right now and how we've been able to solve those? Would that be more helpful to him?"
7. "So besides [X], who else do you feel we could possibly help?"

**Calling a referral (problem-focused, never assume interest):**
- "Hi, is this [John]? This is [Name]. A mutual friend/business associate of yours, [Amy], suggested I call you, as I recently helped her with [X], which was causing [XYZ], and she mentioned you might be experiencing the same challenges with that. Is this an appropriate time to talk?"
- (If meeting a referral in person) "[Name], it's nice to meet you, and let's do this… just so I don't go over things you've already talked about with [Jim], perhaps you can give me your thoughts on what you discussed with him and then what you'd like to cover, so we could focus on you and what you might be looking for?"

> "Might be" is the neutral term — most people don't think they have a problem yet. Don't assert they have the same problem ("she said you're having the same issues") — A-type personalities will push back and resistance goes up.

## CUSTOMER RETENTION — NEPQ Questions To Keep Customers

Monitor the relationship; the answers tell you if you're at risk of losing them.
- "How do you feel we are helping you the most? Why though? What type of changes do you feel we could make to help you achieve even greater results?"
- "What objectives would you like to see us help you get to within the next quarter/year?"
- "How do you see us being different than the former vendor you were working with? Why do you feel like we are, though?"
- "How could we make your life easier? Tell me more…"
- "In what ways do you feel we could improve that would help you on your end?"
- "Would you be opposed to giving me some advice on how we could do better for you?"

## COMPLAINT HANDLING — NEPQ Resolving Questions

When a customer complains, don't argue — ask questions that let them work through it with you:
- "What happened exactly, just so I know?"
- "Can you say more about that so I understand?"
- "I appreciate you bringing this to my attention. Can you tell me everything you know about what happened?"
- "How do you think it got to this point?"
- "I apologize that this happened. What would you like to see done at this point so we can correct this for you?"
- "Would it help you if I did some fact-finding and then we got back together to discuss some possible actions that could take care of this for you?"
- "If anything else happens in the meantime, can you come to me so I can help you?"

## The two non-negotiables (carry into every question above)
1. **Tone + pace are part of the question.** Honor the `...` pauses and the tone notes (curious / concerned / skeptical / playful / challenging). The words alone don't lower resistance — the calm, neutral delivery does.
2. **Repeat their words back.** Almost every bracketed `[repeat back…]` is the engine: the prospect is persuaded by hearing their own situation, gap, and desired outcome reflected to them.
